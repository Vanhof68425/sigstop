# eps2.0_make-and-move.txt

World 2. Things are different now, so listen.

For seven episodes you were a ghost. You looked, you read, you searched,
and you changed nothing — one empty signal file at a time, that's all I
let you leave behind. There was a reason. An operator who can't touch
anything can't destroy anything. You were safe because you were
harmless.

Tonight you get hands.

`mkdir` builds. `touch` creates. `cp` duplicates. `mv` relocates and
renames. And `rm` —

we'll get to `rm`.

Here's the job. The Reyes evidence — the three 4471 files you recovered
— is sitting in a pile in your incoming directory, exactly as it was
pulled off the mirror. A pile is not evidence. A pile is a liability
with no structure, no naming convention, and no way to prove chain of
custody. Before SIGSTOP can move against the launch, this material goes
into a proper vault: built by you, organized by you, named by you,
verified by you.

Two doctrines for the new world:

**Copy, then verify, then touch the original.** `cp` leaves the source
in place. `mv` doesn't. Until a copy is confirmed, the original is
sacred. Apex lost half their own audit trail because someone moved when
they should have copied.

**Deletion is a decision, not a reflex.** You'll understand tonight.

Click **START**.
