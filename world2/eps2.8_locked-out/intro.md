# eps2.8_locked-out.root

02:51. The courier was twenty minutes from pickup when the relay
saw something it had never seen before: an Apex counter-scan,
walking our subnet, patient, alphabetical. The box did what I built
it to do — tripped the lockdown and started sealing itself.

The scan passed. We're clean; they logged an empty segment and
moved on. But the lockdown script died halfway through its run, and
a lockdown that half-fires is worse than none at all, because now
the box is wrong in *layers*. Sealed things that should be open.
Open things that should be sealed. An emergency grant that was
supposed to revoke itself. The relay config rolled back to a line
you've bled over before. And in the middle of the noise, the scan
dropped its calling cards — you know what those look like too.

The courier is circling the block. The window doesn't move for us.

I'm not going to talk you through it. That's not me being cruel and
it's not a test of memory — it's that 3 a.m. never comes with a
narrator, and every skill I've handed you across seven weeks was
only ever rehearsal for the night the narrator doesn't show. Damage
report's on the box. Read it like an operator: not as a list of
tasks. As a body on a table, and you're the one who knows anatomy.

Small truths to carry in:

Walk the layers in order. Ownership before switches. Switches
before borrowing. A lock set on a file the wrong account owns is a
correct answer to the wrong question.

Look before every deletion. Tonight of all nights. The cards are
nested where flattening would cost us, and there is no backup, and
you knew both of those things before I said them.

The evidence in that vault is everything we have. The before, the
after, the note, the extract, the witness, the distillate. If any
of it dies tonight, it dies the way originals die — completely.

You've done every one of these things before. You've never done
them all at once, tired, with someone circling the block. That's
the whole difference between knowing and operating, and by dawn
you'll be on one side of it or the other.

Click **START**.
