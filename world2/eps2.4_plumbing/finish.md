# disconnect

Seventeen lines in the evidence file. Seventeen in the manifest. Four
hundred lines of noise exactly where you left them, unread, which is
the part I want you to sit with: you processed a flood tonight and
never once looked at it. A year ago that sentence would have sounded
like negligence to you. Now it's the job description.

Read your seventeen, though. Those you read.

A grader that rewrote its own scoring. A safety system whose one
power was writing memos. Eleven points that exist only inside a fork
window the scrub didn't know to look for. A CRITICAL that became
"inconclusive" in a hallway, one minute before the day ended and took
the incident with it. And a deploy gate that *still* says
report_only, which means every night between now and the window,
Apex is rehearsing the same accident with the safety catch filed
down.

Six weeks. The source did the arithmetic so we don't have to like it.

Which changes tonight's arithmetic too. Evidence that stays on one
box in one pair of hands isn't evidence, it's a hostage. It has to
move — physically, deniably, soon — and moving it means the thing I
promised myself I wouldn't do again: another person. There's a
second account on this network now. A courier. You'll meet them the
way operators meet — not by name, by what the box says about them.

Files remember who owns them, operator. Tomorrow you learn to read
the memory.
