# disconnect

The relay is up. Burn-on-read is on. And for the first time since
February, our infrastructure isn't introducing us to strangers.

Notice what the fix cost: three lines. Notice what the breach cost:
the cache, the beacons, an evening of precision deletion, and — if
you'd been half a second faster with the hammer in eps2.1 — a witness
statement that ends careers. That's the exchange rate between editing
a config and not editing a config. It never gets more favorable than
tonight. It only gets worse.

You're also, quietly, a different kind of user now. Before tonight,
files were things that happened *to* you — you read them, you copied
them, you deleted them from the outside. Tonight you sat inside one
and changed what it said. Hold the door handles loosely: nano is the
training scalpel, and one night soon you'll meet the operating theater
everyone jokes about. People spend whole careers being afraid of it.

You'll spend one episode.
