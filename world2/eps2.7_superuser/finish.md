# disconnect

`burned by: courier`.

Four words on a root-owned receipt, and every one of them is
tradecraft. The burn needed root; the courier never *had* root —
they had one narrow promise in a ledger you proofread, and for four
seconds the box honored it and then took it back. That's the entire
theory of power in this collective: borrowed, bounded, logged,
returned. The other theory was sitting in the same directory —
`ALL`, no password, since install — and you deleted it, which means
tonight this box has exactly one story about privilege and you can
recite it from memory.

You also stood in the denied seat. I made you cross the room for a
reason. Root can't feel a lock any more than weather can feel a
wall, and an operator who has never been told *no* by a box starts
mistaking absence of resistance for skill. The courier's seat is
where most of the world sits. Every switch you'll ever set is a
sentence addressed to someone in that chair. Now you've been the
recipient. Set your locks accordingly.

The bag is burned. The evidence is moving. The ledger is narrow.
World 2 has one night left, and I'll be honest with you the way I
wasn't before: the last night is not shaped like teaching. Something is going to be wrong on that box — several
somethings, tangled, the way real 3 a.m. looks — and the only
doctrine left to teach you is the one nobody can hand over: walk
the failures in order, smallest working layer first, and trust your
hands.

Everything in World 2 was rehearsal. Next transmission is the
performance.
