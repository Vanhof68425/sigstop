# eps2.6_permission-denied.mod

Since your first night on this network there has been a door you
couldn't open. `/opt/apex`. You found it in World 1. You ran your
`ls` against it and the box said no, and you moved on, and every
episode since, it's been sitting there in the corner of your eye.
I know it has. I check what you look at.

Tonight I explain the lock. Then I hand you the key. Then — pay
attention to the order — I watch you lock it again.

First, the switches. Yesterday you read the two names on a file.
Tonight, the nine letters beside them: three powers, three times.
**r** — read it. **w** — change it. **x** — run it; on a directory,
*enter* it. Three switches for the owner. The same three for the
group. The same three for everyone else on earth. That's the whole
system. Every "permission denied" you will ever see for the rest of
your life is one of those nine switches sitting open, and I mean
every — the industry sells armored racks of security product, and
underneath the invoices it is still nine switches, r-w-x, three
times, guarding everything from your photographs to the launch
codes, maintained by people who set them once at 3 a.m. and never
looked again.

`chmod` flips them. Letters for one switch — `+x`, and a script
becomes a tool. Numbers for the whole row at once: read is 4, write
is 2, execute is 1, add them per column. `750` — owner everything,
group reads and enters, strangers starve. `640` — owner reads and
writes, group reads, strangers starve. `000` — everyone starves,
even the owner. You've seen that one. You've been walking past it
for weeks.

So. What's behind the door: a mirror of Apex's deployment tree,
captured the night you breached them in World 1. I sealed it before
you ever saw the inside — not because you'd have broken something.
Because evidence you look at casually becomes furniture, and I
needed you to *want* the door before I let you through it. That was
manipulative. I'd do it again.

Behind it is the file that decides how Prometheus ships. Go read
what the gate says about the safety-shim. It's one line, and it's
the reason this collective exists.

Open. Read. Seal it behind you. That's how a professional touches a
wound.

Click **START**.
