# eps1.4_needle.grep

We pulled Apex's internal chat. Don't ask how. Three channels, months of
history, forty thousand lines of engineers talking about lunch, deploys,
lunch again, and — six times, scattered across all of it — the thing they
were told to stop talking about.

Eval 4471. The run you read about in the dossier. The one Reyes flagged
three times before they walked her out.

Here's what I want you to understand about forty thousand lines: nobody
reads them. Not Apex's own security team, not the auditors, not the
lawyers who subpoenaed a copy and billed four hundred hours for a
paralegal to skim it. Volume is where corporations hide things from
themselves. It works because reading is the only tool most people have.

You have a better one. `grep` doesn't read. It interrogates. You give it
a pattern and a place, and it returns every line that matches and
nothing else. Forty thousand lines become six. The haystack was never
the problem. The problem was people kept using their eyes.

Somewhere in those six lines, Reyes left us a codeword. She knew exactly
one kind of person would ever find it: someone who greps.

Be that person.

Click **START**.
