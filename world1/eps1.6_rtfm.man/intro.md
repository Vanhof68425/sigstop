# eps1.6_rtfm.man

Last lesson before the job. It's also the last lesson I'll ever *need*
to give you, because tonight I'm replacing myself.

Every command on this box ships with its own documentation. `man ls`.
`man grep`. `man find`. Complete, precise, written by the people who
built the tool, installed on every Linux machine on the planet, and
read by almost no one. Engineers will search the internet, ask a
coworker, ask a language model, guess, give up — anything to avoid the
manual sitting two keystrokes away. Reading it has been an insult for
decades. RTFM. As if pointing someone at the truth were rude.

Here's the operator's view: the manual is the only teacher that can't
be raided, subpoenaed, offboarded, or made to forget. Reyes is gone.
One day this channel goes quiet too. The manual stays.

So tonight I give you a task and *no tool name*. Somewhere in the
manual for a command you already know, there's a capability you've
never used. The task is impossible without it and trivial with it.
You'll find it the way operators find everything: by reading what was
in front of you the whole time.

Inside `man`, you're actually inside `less` — you already know how to
move and how to `/` search. That wasn't an accident. Nothing in this
curriculum was.

Click **START**.
