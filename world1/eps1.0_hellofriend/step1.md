First rule, and it never stops being the first rule: **know where you are and
who you are before you touch anything.** The ones who skipped that step
aren't around to warn you.

Two questions. Ask the machine, not me:

1. Who are you on this box?
2. Where are you standing in the filesystem?

When you know both, I left you something. It's sitting right where you're
standing. Open it and do what it says.

`whoami` tells you who the box thinks you are. `pwd` — print working
directory — tells you where you're standing. Then you'll want to see what's
lying around you. `ls` lists it. Three commands, no flags, no arguments.

</details>
