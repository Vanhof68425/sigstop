Hello, friend.

Apex Cognition is forty-one days from shipping a model they don't understand
to people who won't ask. Their safety team quit in March. It didn't make the
news. You already know why.

We are SIGSTOP. Named for the signal that pauses a process without killing
it. We're not destroyers. We're the emergency brake. And we need operators —
people who know their way around a terminal, who check twice and execute
once, who read the logs nobody else reads.

You're not one of those people yet.

That's what this is for.

---

## What this actually is

SIGSTOP is a free, story-driven series of CTF-style terminal challenges that
teach Linux and DevOps fundamentals to total beginners. Each **episode**
teaches one concept inside a running Linux environment in your browser — no
installation, no setup, no prior experience.

You play a talented, isolated engineer recruited by a paranoid collective to
slow down an AI lab racing to ship an unsafe model. The skills are real. The
terminal is real. The corporation, thankfully, is not.

## How to play

Episodes run on [KillerCoda](https://killercoda.com) — click an episode link
in [LEVELMAP.md](LEVELMAP.md) and it boots a live box in your browser.

Stuck? Every episode has a hint system: `> ping handler` for a nudge,
`>> ping again` for the tool, `>>> just tell me` for the answer. Bosses only
answer the first two pings. That's the point.
