You came back. I logged the exact moment you decided to. 11:47 your time —
you hovered, you thought about your inbox, and you opened the terminal
anyway. Good. Hesitation I can work with. Comfort I can't.

Tonight you learn to move.

A filesystem is a city. Directories are streets. And there are two ways to
give someone an address. An **absolute path** starts from the root of
everything — `/var/log/apex` — it works no matter where you're standing.
A **relative path** — `../logs/apex` — only means something from where you
currently are. Apex engineers mix them up constantly. It's how they deploy
to the wrong environment. It's how, one day soon, it'll be how they lose
something important.

You will not mix them up. Doctrine: **before you move, know where you are.
After you move, confirm where you landed.** `pwd` is not a beginner
command. It's a survival habit. I still run it. Every time.
