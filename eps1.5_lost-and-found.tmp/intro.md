# eps1.5_lost-and-found.tmp

Think about what Reyes was up against in her last week. She knew the
scrub was coming. She knew every directory she was *supposed* to have
access to would be imaged, audited, wiped. So she did the only rational
thing a paranoid person can do with evidence: she made copies and threw
them into corners of the filesystem no audit would think to check. A
config directory here. A library path there. Places files go to be
forgotten.

Then she walked out without writing down a single location.

That's not carelessness. That's tradecraft. A list of hiding places is
just a confession with a table of contents. Instead she did what
operators do: she trusted the *naming*. Every copy carries the eval
number in its filename. `4471_` something. She didn't need to remember
where they were, because she knew that anyone worth reaching would own
the one tool that turns a filename into a location.

`find`. You met it in the job — no. You haven't done the job yet. Forget
I said that. `find` walks every directory from wherever you point it,
checks every name against your pattern, and reports back. The filesystem
stops being a place you search and becomes a question you ask.

Three copies survived the scrub. Find all three. Read them all — the
last piece of the 4471 story is spread across them, and so is your
codeword.
